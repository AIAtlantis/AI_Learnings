Agentic AI landscape classifier — recruiter quick-start guide
A talent intelligence tool for sourcing, benchmarking, and mapping AI/ML roles across the full agentic AI stack

What this is
This classifier organizes the agentic AI ecosystem into 7 tiers — from core foundational model labs at the top to local AI boutiques at the bottom. Each entity is tagged with its industry, product, estimated usage scale, tech stack, and workforce geography. Use it to orient your sourcing strategy, qualify target companies for talent mapping, and understand where specific skills live in the market.

The 7 tiers at a glance
Tier 1 — Foundational
Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Meta, Mistral. Builds base models. Highest technical bar — research scientists, pre-training engineers, alignment researchers.
Tier 2 — Orchestration
LangChain, LlamaIndex, CrewAI, AutoGen. Framework builders. Strong demand for ML engineers who also ship production Python.
Tier 3 — Hyperscaler
AWS, Azure, GCP, Salesforce. Cloud-native agentic platforms. Largest headcount. Sourcing pools in Seattle, Redmond, Sunnyvale, Bangalore.
Tier 4 — Enterprise Apps
Harvey, Glean, Abridge, Moveworks. Vertical SaaS. Fastest-growing talent demand. Mix of applied ML, domain expertise, and enterprise GTM.
Tier 5 — Mid-Market
Cursor, Perplexity, Jasper, Synthesia. Funded startups with focused products. Strong passive sourcing targets — high mobility, skill transferability.
Tier 6 — SMB/No-Code
Zapier, Voiceflow, Botpress, n8n. Workflow automation layer. Source here for AI product managers, integration specialists, and prompt engineers.
Tier 7 — Local AI
Boutiques, consultants, small agencies. Candidates here are often self-taught, highly adaptive, and underrepresented in traditional sourcing pipelines.
How to use the filters
By tier
Start here when you know the seniority or technical depth of the role. Tier 1–2 for research-heavy roles; Tier 4–5 for applied AI; Tier 6–7 for AI-adjacent and generalist talent.

By industry
Filter to a vertical — Legal, Healthcare, Automation — to identify which companies your passive candidates are likely working at today. Useful for building target company lists.

By stack
Match a required skill (Python, RAG, LangChain, TypeScript) to every company using it. Quickly identifies which tiers hold the deepest concentration of that skill.

By region
Scope to North America or Europe for geo-constrained roles or relocation sourcing. Expand each card to see city-level workforce distribution.

Reading a company card
The industry badge (top right) tells you the vertical — useful for assessing domain transferability
Usage and revenue signal company maturity and likely comp band — calibrate your offers accordingly
HQ is the primary office; click Details ▼ to see distributed workforce cities — these are your true sourcing geographies
Tech stack pills map directly to Boolean strings and LinkedIn skill filters
Employee count gives you a rough sense of org size and role availability
Talent intelligence use cases
Build target company lists for passive sourcing campaigns by tier and stack
Identify skill adjacencies — a candidate from Tier 5 may be a strong fit for a Tier 4 enterprise role
Benchmark comp and seniority expectations by tier before making offers
Map workforce geographies to inform office-based vs. remote sourcing strategy
Use industry filters to pitch domain-relevant roles to passive candidates
Spot talent density — stacks like Python, RAG, and LangChain appear across all tiers, signaling broad sourcing pools
